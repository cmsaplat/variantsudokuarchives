Welcome to the Variant Sudoku Archives!

Perhaps you have seen a Sudoku with numbers outside of the grid, or Sudokus with squiggly green or magenta lines, and these unexplained symbols have caused you some great confusion and intimidation. You already need to fill a 9x9 grid with a whopping 81 numbers--and now you have to worry about drawings too?!

Thankfully, these symbols really aren't as scary as an out-of-context peek may make them appear to be. In reality, these symbols are usually the mark of aÂ Sudoku Variant--which is usually a puzzle that follows some classic sudoku rules (if you're unfamiliar with them see Classic Sudoku Rules), with a few extra rules that you wouldn't see in its traditional form. Sudoku players love these variants--they show up in online puzzle hunts, puzzle community servers, and even Sudoku world championships!

This website aims to introduce newer players to these variants through approachable Sudokus (all set by the lovely clover!), giving brief descriptions and tips for the most commonly found Sudoku variants.

Please note that the variants on this website are not a comprehensive list of all Sudoku variants--they simply describe the most common variants and/or the basis for more unique ones.
